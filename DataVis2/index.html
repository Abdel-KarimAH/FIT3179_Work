A point that needs to be taken into consideration is that some areas around the world consist of more flights per day than others. Additionally, not all continents have the same number of countries which explains why the total number of crashes in a continent may be significantly larger than others.

Keywords Associated With A Crash

The dataset used to build this web page consisted of a summary column that contained sentences for every crash. The text in the data represented either communication or conversations that occurred between pilots during the flight. It could also include information that was extracted from the black box of the plane which has information on every action the pilots took and readings of different sensors. This idiom helps us identify any common theme between crashes that occurred from 1908-2020 and identify the main causes of these crashes based on the number of time a word from the summary appeared in the dataset.
Based on the world cloud idiom, the larger the word displayed is, the more repeated it was in the data. By looking at the idiom, we see that the more relevant and most repeated words include aircraft, landing, control, weather, engine and fuel. The common theme that we see between most these words is that they relate to the aircraft itself and not a country or flight operator. This is not strange, as when most crashes occur it is usually a fault in the aircraft. Which could consist of a failure in landing gear, engine or other mechanical components within the aircraft. This could also be a result of poor maintenance done to the aircraft that is irregularly done.
